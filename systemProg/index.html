CSCI 6202: Computer Systems Fundamentals

Fall 2025 | Wednesday 06:10--08:40PM ET | SEH 4040

Instructor: Dr. Shiza Ali, The George Washington University

This course will focus on teaching the fundamentals of systems programming, using C.

Course Description

On completion of this class, students will:

  • Design and implement C programs using the associated development tools.
  • Understand basic means of constructing applications from libraries and communication with services.
  • Demonstrate an understanding of composition as a fundamental design concept for advanced functionalities.
  • Understand representations of programs and processes.
  • Apply UNIX abstractions for processes, process control, inter-process communication, and file systems.

Fundamentally, students will understand that processes are manipulable data, processes control and manage other processes, and applications are composed out of libraries and processes.

Structure

  • This class is broken into two main activities: lectures and labs.
  • Lectures discuss main concepts in systems with practical implementation details.
  • Labs involve hands-on experience, homeworks preview, and practicing key mechanisms.
  • Assignments include a series of homeworks (in C) testing your ability to apply course concepts in a set of projects.
  • In-class coding exercises will help you learn nuances of certain topics.
